Her Story

A life rich in adventure, creativity, and connection

Dee was born in Toronto to Prince Constantine Michael Nakashidze and Beatrice Josephine Villiers Nakashidze, an opera singer in Canada. After graduating from Ryerson Institute of Technology, she set out at twenty-one on a grand tour of Africa. The journey became three remarkable years, including work writing for the South African BBC.

Returning to Canada, she married Septimus Van der Linden and began a family life that carried her through Canada, the United States, Puerto Rico, and Australia. In 1979 she married Holbrook “Hal” Bugbee. They settled in Westport, Connecticut, before retiring — in Dee's energetic sense of the word — to Vero Beach, Florida, in 2000.

A Creative Spirit

Dee made life more vivid

Whatever the medium, her instinct was to make, gather, delight, and share.

✒

Words & performance

She wrote for radio as Deidre Dineen, led public relations for a local bank, edited the Queen City Savings newsletter, and wrote and directed comedy for the Racquets Club Players.

✣

Art in every form

Oil painting was only the beginning: tile mosaic, stained glass, illustration, décor, and writing all belonged to her creative world. She painted prolifically until 2022.

❦

The art of gathering

Trained at the Cordon Bleu Institute, Dee was a consummate hostess whose themed parties were legendary — as was her gift for taking your photograph at the exact moment your mouth was full.

“Boundless creativity, quirky humour, and a deep love of whimsy.”

Her People

The family and communities she loved

Family

Dee is lovingly remembered by her husband, Holbrook Bugbee; her children, Michael, Tira, and Sean Van der Linden; and her stepchildren, Doug Bugbee and Sandy Larsen.

Her grandchildren — Scott, Courtney, Jessica, Alexandra, Ryan, Georgia, Zoe, Oliver, Naomi, and Will — carry forward the laughter, curiosity, and generosity with which she met the world.

She joins her parents and her sisters, Zoya Carr-Harris and Darijan Merry.

Community

President of the Westport Women's Club and Westport Arts Club; volunteer at the Vero Beach Museum of Art; member and exhibitor with the Vero Beach Art Club.

Always nearby

Her gardens, with which she conversed — and a succession of beloved poodles: Was, Isabelle “Izzy,” To-be, Whatnot, and Sterling.

Her remarkable life remains a testament to the gifts of joy and beauty she shared so generously.

Two Worlds

Where the Lines Meet

On her father's side, Deirdre descended from the princely houses of western Georgia — the Nakashidze of Guria, joined by marriage to the Mikeladze, Nizharadze, Marshania, Daniani, Tsulukidze, Shervashidze, Dadiani and Dadeshkeliani families: the great names of Guria, Samegrelo, Abkhazia and Svaneti. Her grandfather Mikhail Mikhailovich Nakashidze built the family house on Ninoshvili Street.

On her mother's side ran a British and Irish inheritance — the Sankey family with the O'Hara, Roe and Clarke lines of Ireland, and the Scottish Ponton, Nisbet and Patterson families. The two worlds met in the marriage of Prince Constantine Michael Nakashidze and Beatrice Josephine Villiers Sankey, and their children — Deirdre, Zoya and Darijan — carried both.
