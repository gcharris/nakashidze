The North American Branch

Deirdre's place in the family record

Deirdre Nakashidze Bugbee (1933–2026) was a granddaughter of Prince Mikhail Mikhailovich Nakashidze, who built the family house on Ninoshvili Street in Batumi. The Toronto gathering remembers Dee while the plate brings her Georgian inheritance to the wider family gathered there.

The Georgian Connection

Where the family lines meet

On her father's side, Dee descended from the princely houses of western Georgia — the Nakashidze of Guria, joined by marriage to the Mikeladze, Nizharadze, Marshania, Daniani, Tsulukidze, Shervashidze, Dadiani and Dadeshkeliani families: the great names of Guria, Samegrelo, Abkhazia and Svaneti. Her grandfather Mikhail Mikhailovich Nakashidze built the family house on Ninoshvili Street. The commemorative plate carries the arms of this shared Georgian line.

On her mother's side ran a British and Irish inheritance — the Sankey family with the O'Hara, Roe and Clarke lines of Ireland, and the Scottish Ponton, Nisbet and Patterson families. The two worlds met in the marriage of Prince Constantine Michael Nakashidze and Beatrice Josephine Villiers Sankey, and their children — Deirdre, Zoya and Darijan — carried both.
